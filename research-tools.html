Library Programs and Learning

Research Opportunities

  • Kids Can Research Anything!
    • Does your child have a topic they are interested in learning more about? The library is a great place to explore any topic! Here are the steps to getting started.
  • Research Steps

    1. Research Steps

      First, students need to pick a topic they are interested in. They need to think of at least 5 good questions they have about this topic to guide their research.

    2. Collect Sources

      Next, they need to check out books about their topic and search the internet for the answers to their questions. Our library website has plenty of safe research websites they can explore.

    3. Record Their Findings

      Using any writing tool, students need to record the answer to their questions. They need to take notes on the important information they learned.

    4. Share Their Learning

      After they have finished recording their notes, they need to pick a way to display their information. They can create a slideshow, poster board, video, diorama, and more! They can choose any creative way to display their learning to share with their classmates. After they are finished, they will have an opportunity to talk about their project. This is a great opportunity for students to collaborate with others as well!
